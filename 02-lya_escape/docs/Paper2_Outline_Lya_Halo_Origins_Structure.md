# Paper 2 — The Origins & Structure of Lyα Halos

## Working outline · Intro → Data → Methods → Results → Discussion

*Intentionally lighter and less defined than Paper 1. Same manuscript skeleton, fewer
locked decisions. This paper inherits Paper 1's pipeline and phenomenology and turns to
the question Paper 1 deliberately left open: **where does the halo light come from?***

**Working title (placeholder):** *The Origins and Structure of Lyα Halos: Escape,
Spatial Profiles, and Environment*

**Driver:** the **Lyα escape fraction** and the **core ↔ halo flux relation.** Does the
halo flux track the core flux (escape / scattering of core-produced photons) or decouple
from it (in-situ production — satellites, cold accretion, fluorescence)? That relation is
the fork the whole paper turns on.

**Inherited boundary:** Paper 1 is everything measurable from the spectra without a model.
Paper 2 is everything that requires decomposing the light into components (scattered vs
in-situ) or forward-modeling the RT — escape fraction, two-halo term, N_HI / v_out fields.

## 3. Methods (light)
- **Lyα escape fraction** (core, halo, total): definition; measured against an
  Hα/Hβ-inferred intrinsic Lyα with dust correction; per-radius and per-subsample.
- **Core-to-halo flux fraction** (spatial escape): how much core light ends up at large
  radius — the scattering budget.
- **Two-halo term** in the SB profile: fit / identify the large-radius upturn in the flux
  curve as a clustering / satellite contribution.
- **Deeper subsample splits**: extend the Paper 1 splits with the larger sample.
- **Overdensity estimation**: method for local environment density.
- **Burstiness diagnostics**: multi-timescale SFR; carry the scope-doc caveat that the
  matched radii are PSF-dominated and timescales reach only ~0.3 R_vir → differential
  study with the 2× data.
- **Full RT forward-fitting** (N_HI, v_out per bin): the Byrohl collaboration; maps the
  Paper 1 model-agnostic observables onto physical fields.

## 5. Discussion (light)
- Resolve, as far as the data + model allow, **scattering vs in-situ**.

- Compare to **cosmological RT simulations** (the natural quantitative targets).
- Synthesis with Paper 1: what the combined picture says about CGM gas flows.
