## Literature scan

Checked against all current filenames in `docs/literature/` (52 files) to avoid repeats, including everything already surfaced in the 07-10/07-13/07-17 briefings (Smirnov 2026, Kikuta 2023 — now actually in the library — Claeyssens 2022, Verhamme 2018 remain open suggestions from last week, not re-listed here to avoid redundancy). Weighted toward this week's one-halo/two-halo/core-boundary decomposition theme (Part 3b) and the ongoing centroid/outflow-inflow interpretation question from `GOALS.md`.

**Recent (2025–2026):**

1. **Sebastian 2026** — Connecting CGM enrichment with Lyman α emitters at 2.9 < z < 6.7 (arXiv:2605.20622, May 2026). Blind MUSE search for 156 LAEs across three deep quasar fields, cross-matched against C IV/Mg II CGM absorbers from the E-XQR-30 survey at impact parameters < 250 pkpc. A direct empirical handle on the same one-halo/CGM-vs-large-scale-structure question Part 3b is now built to quantify, from the absorption side rather than the emission-stacking side.
2. **Li 2025** (Li & Zheng) — Emulating extended Lyman-alpha haloes around star-forming galaxies (arXiv:2508.10095, MNRAS, Aug 2025). Builds Gaussian-process emulators over radiative-transfer-simulated Lyα surface-brightness profiles for three source types (central SF, satellite SF, CGM recombination), then fits mock profiles to recover model parameters. Relevant less for its results than its method: an independent forward-modeling approach to disentangling exactly which physical component (central vs. satellite vs. recombination) a stacked profile's halo zone reflects — the same question Part 3b's core/halo/2-halo split is trying to answer empirically rather than via full RT.
3. **Gagnon-Hartman 2026** — Characterizing Lyman alpha emission from high-redshift galaxies (arXiv:2602.13389, Feb 2026). Flexible, data-driven empirical model for the emergent Lyα profile (post-ISM/CGM, pre-IGM), conditioning Lyα luminosity distributions on UV magnitude. A useful reference point for the UV-vs-Lyα scale-ratio work (currently ≈10:1) if the project ever wants to frame that ratio against a UV-magnitude-conditioned population model rather than just individual literature ratios (Kikuta 2023, LaCOS II).

**Older, likely not yet reviewed (2014, ~12 years back — outside the usual 5–10yr window but foundational for the exponential-scale-length method this project uses):**

4. **Momose 2014** — Diffuse Lyα Halos around Galaxies at z=2.2-6.6: Implications for Galaxy Formation and Cosmic Reionization (arXiv:1403.0732, 2014). Stacks 100–3600 LAEs per redshift bin (z=2.2 to 6.6) from Subaru narrowband imaging, explicitly tests and rules out large-scale PSF artifacts as an explanation for the diffuse halos (a validation step directly parallel to this project's own star-PSF comparison), and fits single-exponential scale lengths across five redshift bins. A useful multi-redshift external comparison set for the h1≈16-17 kpc result, and a template for the PSF-artifact-rejection test if it's ever worth re-stating explicitly in the Methods section.
