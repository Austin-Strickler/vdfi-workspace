## Literature scan

Checked against all 47 filenames in `docs/papers/`, plus the 5 papers already surfaced (but not yet added to the library) in last week's `2026-07-10.md` briefing, to avoid repeating either.

**Recent (2025–2026):**

1. **Sebastian 2026** — Connecting CGM enrichment with Lyman α emitters at 2.9<z<6.7 (arXiv:2605.20622, MNRAS, May 2026). Blind MUSE search for 156 LAEs in three z~6 quasar fields, cross-matched against CIV/MgII CGM absorbers from the E-XQR-30 survey. Finds a low-mass, faint LAE population is likely polluting the CGM with metals — a different observational angle (absorption + emitter cross-correlation) on the same emitter/CGM connection this project probes via emission-line halos.

2. **Mentuch Cooper 2026** — HETDEX Public Data Release 1: Source Catalog 2 and Data Cubes from ~90 sq deg of Integral-Field Optical Spectroscopy (arXiv:2606.04208, June 2026). The HETDEX survey-description paper for the expanded/reprocessed HPSC2 catalog (426K LAEs, new ML classifiers, four more years of data than HPSC1). Not a halo-science result itself, but likely the reference to cite if this project's sample selection ever moves to the newer catalog.

3. **Carr 2025** — Resolving the Origins and Pathways of Ionizing Radiation Escape with UV Integral Field Spectroscopy (arXiv:2512.24895, Dec 2025). Science case for using UV IFU spectroscopy to trace how ionizing photons escape star-forming galaxies during reionization. Adjacent to `01-lya_halos` proper but squarely in `02-lya_escape` territory, since escape pathways and Lya halo strength are commonly linked in the literature (per the LaCOS papers already flagged last week).

**Older, likely not yet reviewed (past 5–10 years):**

4. **Runnholm 2023** — On the evolution of the size of Lyman alpha haloes across cosmic time: no change in the circumgalactic gas distribution when probed by line emission (arXiv:2304.13057, MNRAS, May 2023). Large multi-survey compilation (including MUSE-HUDF and KBSS-type samples) finding LAH sizes don't evolve once measurement systematics are controlled for — a useful null result to have on hand if this project's own halo-size measurements come out redshift-independent too, since it argues that's not itself surprising.

5. **Wu 2020** — Diffuse Lyman-alpha Halos around ~300 Spectroscopically Confirmed Lyman-alpha Emitters at z~5.7 (arXiv:2002.02029, ApJ, Feb 2020). Stacks 310 spectroscopically confirmed LAEs at z~5.7 and discusses background/sky-subtraction systematics that can artificially suppress or bias faint extended emission during stacking — directly relevant methodological reading given this week's `optimize.py` work on scoring background/continuum noise.
