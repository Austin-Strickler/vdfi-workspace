## Literature scan

Checked against all 45 filenames in `docs/papers/` to exclude anything already reviewed.

**Recent (2025–2026):**

1. **Characterizing Lyman-alpha emission from high-redshift galaxies** (arXiv:2602.13389, submitted Feb 2026). Treats Lyα as a probe of the Epoch of Reionization, focused on how the emergent line profile depends on ISM/CGM composition and geometry. Notes large galaxy-to-galaxy scatter in the emergent spectrum — likely relevant background for interpreting scatter in your own stacks.

2. **The Lyα and Continuum Origins Survey II** (arXiv:2504.07074, accepted Jan 2026). Uses Lyα and UV continuum imaging of 42 nearby galaxies to connect Lyman continuum escape to CGM conditions traced by Lyα halos. Reports the Lyα halo scale length is ~10x the UV scale length on average — a concrete number worth comparing against your own extraction results.

3. **Emulating extended Lyman-alpha haloes around star-forming galaxies** (arXiv:2508.10095, MNRAS, Sept 2025). Builds an emulator for LAH surface-brightness profiles from spherically-symmetric CGM gas models. Methodologically adjacent to your stacking/measure pipeline — could be useful as an independent model to compare stacked profiles against.

**Older, likely not yet reviewed (past 5–10 years):**

4. **The clustering of Lyman Alpha Emitting galaxies at z=2-3** (arXiv:2406.01803, 2024). Uses LAE clustering to constrain typical host halo mass — a different observational route to the same stellar-mass↔halo-mass link your virial radius comparison is working on, useful as a cross-check point.

5. **The Stacked Lyman-Alpha Emission Profile from the Circum-Galactic Medium of z~2 Quasars** (arXiv:1604.02942, 2016). Stacks Lyα CGM emission around z~2 quasars rather than star-forming galaxies — an older paper, but a useful methodological comparison since it's tackling the same stacking-for-diffuse-emission problem in an adjacent population.
