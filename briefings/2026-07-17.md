## Literature scan

Checked against all 50 filenames now in `docs/literature/` (the reference library — note it's `docs/literature/`, not the `docs/papers/` path the older README/template still name), plus the papers already surfaced in the `2026-07-10` and `2026-07-13` briefings (including LaCOS II, Runnholm 2023, Wu 2020), to avoid repeats. Weighted toward this week's UV-continuum-vs-Lyα and profile-fitting focus.

**Recent (2025–2026):**

1. **Smirnov 2026** — Lyman-alpha haloes in the aftermath of reionisation (arXiv:2607.05087, July 2026). MUSE study pushing extended-Lyα-halo characterization to z≳6, testing whether halo properties are imprinted by the tail end of reionisation. A useful high-z bookend to this project's cosmic-noon (z~2–3) sample — the same emission-halo observable at the opposite end of the redshift baseline, and a natural comparison point if/when the subsample analysis touches redshift evolution.

**Older, likely not yet reviewed (past 5–10 years):**

2. **Kikuta 2023** — UV & Lyα halos of Lyα emitters across environments at z=2.84 (arXiv:2302.12848, ApJ, 2023). Stacks 3,490 HSC/Subaru LAEs and fits a **two-component exponential** to both the UV and Lyα radial profiles, recovering scale-lengths of 1.56 ± 0.01 and 10.4 ± 0.3 pkpc and studying how the halo scale-length depends on Mpc-scale environment. Directly parallel to this week's two-exponential `fit_psf_aware` work and the planned UV-continuum comparison — a concrete external UV-vs-Lyα scale ratio and a template for the environment subsample split.

3. **Claeyssens 2022** — The Lensed Lyman-Alpha MUSE Arcs Sample (LLAMAS) I: characterisation of extended Lyα haloes and spatial offsets (arXiv:2201.04674, A&A, 2022). Uses gravitational lensing to reach small physical scales, and finds 60% of galaxies show a significant UV–Lyα spatial offset (median 0.58 ± 0.14 kpc). Relevant to the centroid-vs-radius headline result and to `uv_profile.py`'s kept-but-flagged 2D-Gaussian centroid step — an independent handle on how far the Lyα light can sit from the UV continuum.

4. **Verhamme 2018** — Recovering the systemic redshift of galaxies from their Lyman-alpha line profile (arXiv:1804.01883, MNRAS, 2018). Calibrates the offset between the Lyα line and the true systemic redshift from the line profile itself. Squarely on this project's central methodological advantage (MOSDEF systemic redshifts as the velocity anchor) — worth citing both as the contrast case (profile-inferred vs. independently-measured systemic) and as a cross-check on the size of the red/blue centroid shifts the stack is measuring.
