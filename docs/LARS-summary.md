# The Lyman Alpha Reference Sample (LARS) — Summary

LARS is an HST imaging program (PI Göran Östlin) that observed 14 local star-forming galaxies (z ≈ 0.028–0.19) simultaneously in Lyα, Hα, Hβ, and UV/optical continuum, at ~40 pc resolution. It was designed as a local laboratory for Lyα radiative transfer — the physics that governs how Lyα escapes galaxies at all redshifts, but which can only be resolved spatially in the nearby universe. The sample was later extended to 45 galaxies (eLARS) to widen the range of star formation rates probed. See Östlin et al. 2014, Paper I (survey design, [arXiv:1409.8347](https://arxiv.org/abs/1409.8347)).

## Key conclusions

**Lyα halos are ubiquitous.** Hayes et al. 2013 (ApJL, [arXiv:1303.0006](https://arxiv.org/abs/1303.0006)) showed that essentially every LARS galaxy is embedded in a diffuse Lyα halo roughly twice the linear size of its UV/Hα continuum, and that halos are stronger in galaxies with lower dust content. Hayes et al. 2014, Paper II ([arXiv:1308.6578](https://arxiv.org/abs/1308.6578)) confirmed this with full imaging analysis: Lyα morphology is routinely far less centrally concentrated than Hα or FUV, direct evidence of resonant scattering redistributing photons in the neutral ISM/CGM rather than them escaping along the sightline they were emitted on.

**Escape depends on ISM kinematics and geometry, not just dust.** Rivera-Thorsen et al. 2015, Paper V ([arXiv:1503.01157](https://arxiv.org/abs/1503.01157)) and 2017, Paper VIII ([arXiv:1711.03123](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.03123)) used high-resolution UV spectroscopy and Lyα/Hα/Hβ line-ratio maps to show that the covering fraction and velocity structure of neutral gas — not simply dust content — set how much Lyα escapes and how it's scattered into a halo.

**Halo morphology mimics high-z galaxies.** Guaita et al. 2015, Paper IV, and Rasekh/Melinder et al. 2022, Paper XII ([arXiv:2110.01626](https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.01626)) artificially redshifted the LARS imaging and found LARS galaxies resemble z~2–3 Lyα emitters both in continuum morphology and in halo profile shape — supporting LARS as a genuine local analog for high-z LAEs, not just a superficially similar population.

**Kinematics correlate with Lyα output.** Herenz et al. 2024/2025, Paper XV ([arXiv:2406.03956](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.03956)) used the extended eLARS sample (42 galaxies) to link ionized-gas kinematics (rotating disks vs. perturbed/complex kinematics) with Lyα escape fraction, equivalent width, and luminosity, tying halo strength to dynamical state rather than SFR or dust alone.

**Deep photometry pushes halos further.** Melinder et al. 2018, Paper IX ([arXiv:1803.07207](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.07207)) and Melinder et al. 2023, Paper XIV (already in `docs/papers/`) extended surface-brightness limits and imaged the full 45-galaxy eLARS sample, reinforcing that halos are the norm rather than an exception even at low SFR.

## What's unique about LARS

No other Lyα survey pairs HST-resolution (sub-100 pc) multi-line imaging with a large enough sample to do statistics. High-z Lyα halo work (MUSE, HETDEX, etc.) can measure halo *sizes* but can't resolve the underlying HII regions, dust geometry, or kinematics driving them — LARS can, because it's local. That makes LARS less a "sample of local curiosities" and more a calibration set: it pins down the radiative-transfer physics (scattering, dust, kinematics) that high-z halo/escape-fraction studies have to assume or fit for statistically.

## Legacy / successor survey

The same collaboration (Runnholm, Hayes, Östlin et al.) launched a follow-up in 2025, the **Lyman-alpha and Continuum Origins Survey (LyCOS)** — Paper I, survey description and Lyα imaging ([arXiv:2504.07056](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.07056)), and Paper II, on the journey of escaping Lyα/ionizing radiation through the neutral ISM and CGM ([arXiv:2504.07074](https://arxiv.org/html/2504.07074v1)). LyCOS extends the LARS approach with an explicit CGM/Lyman-continuum focus, effectively the next generation of this local-laboratory strategy.
