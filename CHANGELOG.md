# Changelog

Short, dated log of work across the VDFI workspace. One or two sentences per entry — a change doesn't have to mean a new function (that's what `guide.py` is for). Newest at top.

## 2026-07-13
- Filled in the Background subtraction section of `01-lya_halos/docs/research-notes.md` with three concrete findings: the background annulus radius affects annular-bin height because the sky background itself varies with radius (physically it shouldn't — stars/foreground sources aren't tied to the galaxies being stacked — likely a field/edge-boundary effect); the smoothing window (`config.bg_smooth_sigma_A`, `smoothing.py`) affects outer-bin noise, smaller generally better up to ~30 Å; and a full galaxy-pooled background (`bg_gal` in `extract.py`) beats a per-exposure one, achievable by setting `config.min_bg_fibers` impossibly high so every exposure fails its own-background check and falls back to the pooled galaxy background.
- Added `measure.py`'s `flux_curve_of_growth` (new 4th "derived radial diagnostics" layer, run after Stage 3: cumulative Lyα luminosity and flux fraction vs. radius, built from the already-bootstrapped windowed flux times a representative per-galaxy fiber-footprint area, dropping the noisy outermost bin by default) and `analysis.py`/`plotting.py`'s `plot_flux_curve_of_growth` (two-panel cumulative-luminosity + flux-fraction draw with bootstrap bands); synced into `guide.py`.
- Added `plotting.py`'s `_safe_yerr` helper and wired it through `plot_centroid_vs_radius`, `plot_blue_red_vs_radius`, `plot_centroid_comparison`, `plot_radial_overlay`, and (in `analysis.py`) `plot_centroid_methods`, `plot_flux_profile`, `plot_asymmetry_profile`, `plot_asymmetry_profile_two`: guards against matplotlib's crash on a negative bootstrap-band errorbar when a fiducial point estimate falls outside its own 16–84 band (common in faint/ratio bins), flagging those points with a hollow marker instead of silently clipping.
- Added `docs/Lya-maximum-emission-summary.md` at the workspace root: literature-grounded reference on the theoretical maximum Lyα emission (Case B recombination luminosity ceiling vs. equivalent-width ceiling), for testing observed flux against a physically motivated maximum before invoking a non-stellar photon source.
- `01-lya_halos/docs/subsample_splits.md` landed in git for the first time (previously written but uncommitted): candidate subsample splits assigned to Paper 1 (kinematics) vs. Paper 2 (flux/photon budget), including the double-Gaussian peak-separation split that `research-notes.md`'s noise-prefabrication question stress-tests.
